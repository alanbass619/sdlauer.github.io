An affine plane is a geometry system that follows four axioms given below. Like Fano's geometry, this axiomatic system uses the undefined objects "points" and "lines," and the undefined relation "contains".

Axioms of a finite affine plane:

  1. Given any two distinct points, a unique line contains the points.
  2. Each line contains at least two points.
  3. Given any line and any point not on that line, a unique line exists which contains the point and does not meet the given line.
  4. At least three noncollinear points exist.

The Euclidean plane is an affine plane. However, finite affine planes are also popular systems of geometry.

Theorem: A finite affine plane contains at least four points.
